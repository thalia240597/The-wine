Red Wine

Red wine is made from dark-colored grape varieties and typically has a rich, bold flavor profile. It can range from light and fruity to full-bodied and complex, depending on factors such as grape variety, region, and aging process. Red wines pair well with hearty dishes like red meat, pasta, and aged cheeses.

White Wine

White wine is produced from green or yellowish grapes, and its flavor profile is characterized by its crisp acidity and often fruity or floral aromas. White wines can be dry, sweet, or somewhere in between, offering versatility for various palates and occasions. They pair well with seafood, poultry, salads, and light pasta dishes.

Rosé Wine

Rosé wine, is made from red grape varieties but with a shorter skin contact time during the winemaking process, resulting in its distinct pink hue. Rosé wines typically offer a refreshing and fruity flavor profile, with notes of berries and citrus. They are versatile and can be enjoyed as an aperitif or paired with a wide range of foods, including salads, grilled vegetables, and seafood.
